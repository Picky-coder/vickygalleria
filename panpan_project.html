PanPan Project

Project type: Logotype design

PAN PAN is the name of my imaginary café shop project. I designed this series of logotypes as part of a collection under the PAN PAN concept. Come and explore my logotype designs!

The bread on top

This is the first image I created from my sketches after imagining owning a coffee shop. I envisioned soft bread and hot drinks, which inspired me to create several similar sketches.

A pretty cup

This image was brought to life entirely in vector format, combining the tools of Adobe Illustrator and Ibis Paint. While Adobe Illustrator is powerful, I found its color tools challenging to use. That's where Ibis Paint came to the rescue! Its intuitive and beginner-friendly features made coloring a breeze, helping me turn my vision into reality with ease and joy. Balancing the strengths of these two tools made the process not only manageable but also exciting as I saw the artwork take shape.

Pan Pan Project

PanPan is a name inspired by the Japanese word for bread, 'パン' (pan), reflecting my dream of owning a coffee shop. The vision centers around offering delicious Japanese-style bread as the main attraction, creating a cozy and unique experience for customers.

PanPan in another version

I have a deep fondness for Japanese culture, which greatly inspired the PanPan project. When envisioning this concept, I wanted a logo that truly captured Japan's identity. I imagined animal-shaped bread as a playful representation and transformed that idea into a logo.

PanPan in rabbit version

This version was inspired by an animation about a white talking bear who owned a coffee shop in the North Pole. So, why did I choose a white rabbit instead of the bear from the animation? The answer lies in the personality I envisioned. I thought of the hardworking nature of a worker ant but wanted something fluffy and adorable like a polar bear—just not as giant or intimidating. As I considered dogs, cats, and hamsters, the white rabbit stole my heart and became the perfect choice.
